About

Researcher profile

I work on adsorption-driven environmental technologies with a focus on designing functional porous materials for water treatment, contaminant removal, metal recovery, and carbon capture.

Professional Summary

I am a PhD researcher in Chemical Engineering at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), Trondheim, with an integrated BS–MS background in Environmental Geosciences from IISER Kolkata. My research combines adsorption science, functional porous materials, contaminant transport, and process-oriented environmental engineering to address challenging water and separation problems. Across my doctoral and earlier research work, I have developed and evaluated silica aerogels, mesoporous silica nanoparticles, graphene-derived systems, and mineral-based nanocomposites for PFAS removal, battery metal recovery, chromate remediation, and CO₂ adsorption.

My broader academic profile is defined by the ability to connect material synthesis and characterization with performance testing, mechanistic interpretation, and application-oriented process thinking. I am especially interested in translating high-performance laboratory materials into robust treatment systems relevant to environmental technology, resource recovery, and sustainable industrial practice.

Research Identity

  • Design of advanced porous materials for environmental separations and remediation
  • Adsorption, column studies, and mechanistic understanding of water treatment systems
  • Recovery of critical resources from complex water matrices
  • CO₂ capture using functionalized silica-based material platforms
  • Cross-disciplinary work spanning materials, analysis, transport, and process evaluation
Research

Research themes and academic contributions

My work is organized around a set of connected themes that combine material innovation, separation science, and environmental process relevance.

Water Treatment and Emerging Contaminants

I develop adsorption-based treatment approaches for persistent and difficult-to-remove contaminants, with emphasis on PFAS and multicomponent systems. My work considers not only removal efficiency, but also regeneration, selectivity, long-term stability, and analytical verification in realistic matrices.

PFAS removal Water purification Adsorption systems

Functional Porous Materials

A major focus of my research is the design of mesoporous silica, silica aerogels, graphene-derived sorbents, and hybrid materials with tailored interfacial chemistry. These platforms are used for contaminant capture, separation, and carbon capture, linking material architecture directly to performance.

Silica aerogels Mesoporous silica Hybrid sorbents

Resource Recovery and Process Translation

Beyond removal, I work on recovery of battery-relevant metal ions from complex water matrices and on understanding transport and breakthrough behavior in systems that matter for implementation. This translational perspective is central to how I frame scientific problems and evaluate material systems.

Battery metal recovery Column studies Process relevance
