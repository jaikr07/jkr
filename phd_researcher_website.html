About

Researcher profile

I combine environmental engineering, adsorption science, field-based environmental understanding, and advanced materials development to address difficult water and separation problems with practical relevance.

Professional Summary

I am in the final phase of my PhD in Chemical Engineering at NTNU, following an integrated BS–MS degree in Environmental Geosciences from IISER Kolkata. Across my research journey, I have developed expertise in water quality assessment, contaminant removal, adsorption systems, porous materials, and process analysis. My work includes the design of polymer-functionalized aerogels and mesoporous silica, the development of nanosorbents for metal and PFAS removal, contaminant transport studies in porous systems, and process-level thinking for environmental and industrial implementation. A central thread in my work is the use of silica-based porous materials, including silica aerogels, for both separation and capture challenges spanning water purification and CO₂ adsorption.

Research

Core research themes

My research is centered on designing high-performance material systems and understanding transport, adsorption, and separation behavior in environmentally important water and gas systems.

Water Treatment and Emerging Contaminants

Development of advanced adsorbent systems for the removal of PFAS, metal ions, and other persistent contaminants from challenging water matrices. This includes studies on robustness, cyclic stability, long-term performance, and analytical quantification using modern instrumentation.

PFAS removal Battery metal recovery Water quality

Nanomaterials, Sorbents, and Surface Functionalization

Design and evaluation of mesoporous silica particles, silica aerogels, graphene-derived systems, biochar composites, hydroxyapatite-based materials, and polymer-functionalized interfaces for adsorption, remediation, and separation applications. This includes silica-based porous architectures tailored for both PFAS removal and CO₂ capture.

Mesoporous silica Silica aerogels Graphene systems

Transport, Process Insight, and Scale-Up Thinking

From fixed-bed breakthrough studies and tracer experiments to reactive transport and process evaluation, I focus not only on material performance but also on the system-level behavior required for real implementation in water and environmental engineering contexts.

Reactive transport Column studies Process evaluation
